Hevea
Hevea braziliensis Muell. Arg. 

Hevea, also known as rubber wood, is growing natually in Brazil.
In Malaysia, Thailand and Indonisia it mainly grows in plantations. 

In plantations the trees rarely exceeds 25m in height with a diameter up to 45cm.

The sap in Hevea is light yellowish, and tend to go darker after drying.

The density of the Hevea is on avarage 600 kgs/m3.

The hardness is slightly lower than White Oak. 

Hevea is a stable wood specie with a loss in width of 0.15-0.20% per percent the wood dries.

Hevea is very suitable to make parquet flooring boards from, due to good strenght and high stability combined with high uniformity and good processing properties.

By KTL we purchase the Hevea from plantations on the Malaysian Pinensula. In parquet flooring we offer a clear golden yellowish look, and in a smaller scale also a more rusticial look including black knots and pith.

The products are used in a wide range of applications, from appartments and private homes to commercial floors as well as in Sport facilities.
